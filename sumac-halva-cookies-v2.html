Sumac Halva Cookies
with Verjuice Glaze

Crumbled halva folded through a sumac-spiked dough. The verjuice glaze does what acid always does — makes the richness feel deliberate.

Yield 18–22 cookies
Active 20 min
Total 45 min
Ahead Dough 2 days
The Thinking

Architecture: Tahini in the halva becomes the primary fat. Sumac acts as both flavour and colour, creating the signature Middle Eastern balance between rich and bright. The cookie structure carries the confection, not the other way around.

Pattern: How to build dessert around a confection. Time the glaze — verjuice at the end resolves the sweetness the same way acid resolves fat in a braise.

Flavour Architecture

Fat-led with strong acid counterpoint. The halva's richness is structural — sumac cuts through rather than complementing. Nigella seeds add pop and visual punctuation.

1 Sumac Cookie Base Dough holds 2 days wrapped · Baked cookies 3 days sealed
Ingredients
200 g
plain flour, sifted structure
15 g
sumac, ground fine acid and colour
3 g
nigella seeds, whole texture and visual
120 g
caster sugar, fine crystal sweetness
80 ml
extra virgin olive oil, fruity fat richness
1 large
egg, room temperature binding
3 g
fine sea salt flavour enhancement
150 g
vanilla halva, walnut-sized pieces sweetness and texture
Method
01

Heat oven to 180°C. 20 min preheat

Taste the sumac first — it should be tangy and fruity, not stale or dusty.

02

Whisk flour, sumac, nigella, and salt together in a large bowl. 2 min

Even distribution prevents sumac clumps. The mixture should smell fruity and aromatic.

03

Beat sugar, olive oil, and egg until just combined. 1 min

Minimal mixing. Halva carries enough salt — no more needed here.

04

Fold wet into dry until barely combined. 30 sec

Overworking develops gluten. Dough should taste pleasantly tart.

05

Fold in halva pieces gently. 30 sec

Keep chunks intact. Pieces should be visible and irregularly distributed.

06

Scoop 30 g balls onto lined trays, spaced 4 cm apart. Bake 12–14 min until edges are set and the surface turns matte.

Edges firm before the centre does. The middle looks underdone — correct. It sets on the tray.

Doneness

Dough holds when squeezed but stays slightly crumbly

Sumac evenly distributed, no dry white pockets

Baked: edges golden and set, centre still appears slightly underdone — correct

2 Verjuice Glaze Make at time of glazing only · Does not hold
Ingredients
30 ml
verjuice, slightly reduced acid brightness
120 g
icing sugar, sifted sweetness and body
2 g
nigella seeds finish and garnish
Method
01

Reduce verjuice by half over medium heat. 3 min

Should taste bright but not harsh. Concentrated, not aggressive.

02

Whisk reduced verjuice into icing sugar gradually. 1 min

Balance should favour tart over sweet. It will seem too sharp — correct.

03

Spoon over cooled cookies. Scatter nigella seeds immediately before glaze sets.

Seeds need to adhere — scatter within 30 seconds of glazing.

Doneness

Coats a spoon but drips freely — not stiff, not watery

Pale ivory with slight transparency and a visible sheen

Variations
Orange Blossom

Replace verjuice with orange blossom water in the glaze. Amplifies the halva's natural fragrance. Use sparingly — orange blossom is forceful.

Pistachio

Fold 50 g roughly chopped unsalted pistachios through with the halva. Adds textural relief and colour — the green against the pink of sumac.
